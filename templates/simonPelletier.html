I built and lived in a yurt for one summer in Squamish, BC. Not a very fancy yurt.

Another time I purchased a home with partners in Whitehorse, Yukon. We then split it into two rental units. We gutted the house down to the studs and concrete. More recently I designed and built a tiny home with a former partner of mine. We used locally sourced timber for the inside and outside with a focus on using wood and metal. The home rests on an old trailer I salvaged. Its an off-grid setup with no plumbing and an Amish built wood stove cooktop and a solar system designed by me. It features large windows, white pine shiplap interior with cedar accents and rough white pine board and batten exterior painted with a traditional linseed pine tar paint. It currently sits in the corner of a field tucked against some popular trees on the Birch river. A very quiet spot.
